# Email Copy Patterns Reference

Proven formulas, swipe patterns, and structural templates for email copy elements.

---

## Subject Line Formula Library

### By Emotional Trigger

**Urgency**
- "{Product} is back — {quantity} left"
- "Last {X} hours: {offer}"
- "This ends tonight at midnight"
- "Only {number} spots left for {event/offer}"
- "{Offer} expires in {time}"

**Curiosity**
- "This changed how we think about {topic}"
- "The {topic} mistake almost everyone makes"
- "What {number} of our customers figured out about {topic}"
- "We weren't supposed to share this yet, but..."
- "The real reason {common assumption} doesn't work"

**Benefit / Value**
- "How to {achieve result} in {timeframe}"
- "{Number} ways to {desired outcome} this week"
- "The easiest way to {solve problem} I've found"
- "Cut your {pain point metric} by {percentage}"
- "Get {specific result} without {specific pain}"

**Social Proof**
- "{Number} people grabbed this yesterday"
- "Why {impressive number} teams switched to {product}"
- "What {customer/expert name} said about {topic}"
- "Join {number} others who {achieved result}"

**Personal / Direct**
- "{Name}, quick question about {relevant topic}"
- "I made this for people like you"
- "A personal recommendation for your {situation}"
- "{Name}, you left this behind"

**News / Announcement**
- "Introducing {product/feature}: {one-line benefit}"
- "{Product} just got {specific improvement}"
- "Big news: {announcement in plain language}"
- "We've been working on something for {timeframe}"

**Re-engagement**
- "We miss you (and here's {incentive} to come back)"
- "A lot has changed since you last visited"
- "Should we stop emailing you?"
- "Is this goodbye, {Name}?"

### By Campaign Type

**Back in Stock:**
- "{Product} is back in stock"
- "Your {product} is available again — {quantity} left"
- "It sold out once already. It's back. Grab it."
- "Remember {product}? It's finally here again."
- "{Product} restock alert: limited quantity"

**Abandoned Cart:**
- "Still thinking it over?"
- "Your cart misses you"
- "You forgot something good"
- "Did something go wrong at checkout?"
- "{Product name} is waiting for you"

**Welcome:**
- "Welcome to {brand} — here's your {incentive}"
- "You're in! Here's what happens next"
- "First things first: your {welcome gift}"
- "{Name}, let's get you started"

**Post-Purchase:**
- "Your order is confirmed — here's a quick tip"
- "Your {product} is on its way"
- "Getting the most out of your {product}"
- "How's your {product}? We'd love to know"

**Sale / Promotion:**
- "{X}% off everything — {timeframe} only"
- "Our biggest sale of {period} starts now"
- "Early access: {sale name} for VIP subscribers"
- "{X} hours left to save {percentage}"

## Preview Text Patterns

**Complement the subject (don't repeat):**

| Subject | Preview Text |
|---|---|
| "Your wishlist item is back" | "Only 23 units this time. Move fast." |
| "Still thinking it over?" | "Your cart is saved — here's free shipping to sweeten the deal." |
| "{X}% off starts now" | "Ends Sunday. Here are 3 picks to start with." |
| "Welcome to {brand}" | "Your 15% off code is inside, plus a quick-start tip." |
| "We need your opinion" | "Takes 30 seconds. No essay required." |

**Formulas:**
- Scarcity addition: "{Quantity} remaining. Don't wait on this one."
- Benefit addition: "Inside: {specific value they'll get}."
- Curiosity addition: "The answer surprised us too."
- Proof addition: "{Number} already claimed theirs."
- Time addition: "Offer expires at {specific time}."

## Email Body Templates

### The Problem-Solution Email

```
{Relatable one-line problem statement}

{2-3 sentences expanding on the pain — make it feel real}

{Transition: "That's why we built..." or "Here's what changes that:"}

{Solution in 2-3 sentences — benefit-focused, not feature-focused}

{One proof element — testimonial, stat, or result}

[CTA]

{P.S. — urgency element or secondary benefit}
```

### The Story Email

```
{Open with a scene or moment — "Last Tuesday, one of our customers..."}

{Build the narrative — problem they faced, what they tried}

{The turning point — how your product/service changed things}

{The result — specific, measurable outcome}

{Bridge to the reader — "If you're dealing with something similar..."}

[CTA]
```

### The Social Proof Email

```
{Lead with the proof — number, testimonial, or achievement}

{Context — why this matters}

{2-3 short testimonials or data points}

{Bridge to reader — "Ready to see similar results?"}

[CTA]
```

### The Scarcity / Urgency Email

```
{State the situation — "{X} left" or "{Time} remaining"}

{Remind them of the value — what they'll miss}

{One objection handled — remove the last barrier}

[CTA]

{P.S. — repeat the deadline or quantity}
```

### The Value-First Email (Newsletter / Nurture)

```
{Insight, tip, or useful content — this is the main event}

{Expand with detail, example, or story}

{If relevant, connect to product — soft mention, not hard sell}

{Secondary resource or link}

[Soft CTA or no CTA — trust-building, not conversion-focused]
```

## Power Words for Email Copy

### Urgency
now, today, limited, ending, final, last chance, deadline, expires, closing, running out

### Trust
proven, guaranteed, verified, certified, secure, safe, trusted, backed, endorsed, protected

### Value
free, bonus, save, exclusive, complimentary, included, extra, unlock, access, premium

### Curiosity
secret, hidden, revealed, surprising, unexpected, little-known, insider, behind-the-scenes, untold

### Emotion
love, obsessed, thrilled, frustrated, overwhelmed, relieved, confident, proud, excited, grateful

### Action
grab, claim, unlock, discover, join, start, build, create, transform, master

## Words and Phrases to Avoid

### Spam Trigger Words (overuse hurts deliverability)
- FREE (all caps)
- Act now!!!
- Limited time offer!!!
- Click here
- Buy now
- Don't miss out
- Order now
- While supplies last (unless genuinely true)

### Empty Marketing Speak
- "Innovative solution"
- "Best-in-class"
- "World-class"
- "Cutting-edge"
- "Game-changing"
- "Revolutionary"
- "Synergy"
- "Leverage"

### Trust Killers
- "This is NOT spam"
- "You're receiving this because..."
- Excessive exclamation marks
- ALL CAPS for emphasis
- Fake urgency when there's no real deadline
- Made-up testimonials or statistics
