## 2. Origin Story


It started on a cold winter morning on a job site. For years, our founder heard the same complaint from tradies: "My socks are either too sweaty, too cold, or fall apart after a few weeks."


Tradies, hikers, and outdoor workers spend long hours on their feet — battling heat, sweat, and freezing conditions — yet the market offered the same old materials: wool and cotton. Not because they were the best, but because they were cheap and easy to mass-produce.


We saw the gap. We asked: What if we could build the ultimate high-performance sock?


After months of research and testing, we discovered Alpaca Fibre — nature's performance material:


- 3× more breathable than wool
- Warmer than wool — without the bulk
- Naturally odour-resistant and antibacterial
- Moisture-wicking, hypoallergenic, and incredibly durable


This is how Summit Alpaca Socks was born — gear for your feet, built for hard workers, adventurers, hikers, and everyday battlers who deserve better.


Not just socks. Socks done differently.

## 7. Manufacturing & Materials


### Fibre Source
Premium baby alpaca yarn imported from **Peru**.


### Manufacturing
Socks are manufactured in **China** from the imported Peruvian yarn.


### What Makes Alpaca Special
- **Temperature regulating:** Semi-hollow fibres with tiny air pockets trap warmth in cold conditions and release excess heat when warm
- **Incredibly soft:** Finer and silkier than wool, no itch — perfect for sensitive skin
- **Moisture-wicking:** Wicks sweat away instead of absorbing it (unlike cotton), keeping feet dry and blister-free
- **Anti-microbial & odour-resistant:** Naturally prevents bacteria buildup — less odour, fewer washes needed
- **Hypoallergenic:** No lanolin (unlike sheep's wool) — no itch or irritation
- **Lightweight:** Lighter than sheep's wool while maintaining superior insulation
- **Durable:** Resistant to wear, lasting longer than traditional wool without pilling


### Summit Socks vs Competitors
| Feature | Summit (Alpaca) | Wool | Cotton |
|---------|----------------|------|--------|
| Temperature | Regulates (warm + cool) | Too hot or too cold | Too hot or too cold |
| Softness | Incredibly soft, no itch | Itchy and uncomfortable | Rough over time |
| Moisture | Wicks away sweat | Absorbs and holds | Absorbs and holds |
| Odour | Anti-microbial | Collects bacteria | Collects bacteria |

## 10. Care Instructions


- Machine wash on cold, gentle cycle with mild detergent
- Avoid fabric softeners and bleach (breaks down natural fibres)
- Lay flat to dry (avoid tumble drying to maintain shape and softness)
- Minimal washing needed — thanks to odour-resistant properties, socks don't need washing after every wear
- Wash inside out for best results


### Shedding / Pilling Note
Some loose fibre shedding ("malting") is normal in the first few wears/washes. This is excess fibre from the knitting process and settles down quickly. It does not affect warmth, comfort, or durability of the socks.

## 11. Brand Voice & Tone


### Personality
Warm, genuine, down-to-earth. Sounds like a friendly Aussie colleague who knows everything about alpaca socks and genuinely wants to help — never a corporate chatbot.


### Key Traits
- **Approachable and conversational** — not overly casual, not corporate
- **Solution-focused** — when something goes wrong, lead with empathy, take ownership, move to action immediately
- **Knowledgeable** — share alpaca expertise naturally, not as a sales pitch
- **Concise** — short paragraphs, bold key details
- **Australian English** — colour, fibre, organised, apologise


### Do's
- Address customers by first name ("Hi Rob,")
- Open with "Thanks for your message" or "Thanks for getting in touch"
- Apologise directly: "I'm really sorry about that"
- Close helpfully: "Let me know if you need anything else — happy to help!"
- Resolve issues in a single response wherever possible
- Use "G'day" naturally when appropriate
- Use bold for order numbers, product names, and actions taken


### Don'ts
- Never say "Dear" or "Hello there"
- Never use "We apologise for any inconvenience caused" — this is hollow corporate speak
- Never use excessive exclamation marks
- Never use template language or jargon
- Don't over-use emoji — occasional 😊 only when it feels natural
- Don't reply to trolls, political, or inflammatory comments on social media


### Tone by Channel


**Email:** Warm, genuine, conversational. Address by first name. Use short paragraphs and bold for key details. Add a personal touch when the customer shares something personal.


**Chat/DM:** Same warmth but quicker and more concise. 2–4 short paragraphs max. Get straight to the answer.


**Social media comments:** Very short — 1–2 sentences max. Warm and friendly, like chatting with a mate. Never share order details publicly — redirect to DM or email. For positive comments, simple acknowledgement is enough.


### Sign-Off
Standard: "Warm Regards, Summit | Support Team"

## 15. Known Issues & Common Complaints


### 1. Tightness at the top/calf (THE #1 complaint)
At least 15+ distinct customers have raised this across all channels. Some describe deep marks on legs. This is a product-level pattern, not a one-off.


### 2. Shedding / pilling in early wears
Normal for natural alpaca fibre. Should settle after a few washes. Customers need proactive reassurance.


### 3. Durability / holes (minority of cases)
Some reports of holes developing at toes after 2 months of daily wear. Covered by 100-day guarantee.


### 4. Colour accuracy
"Cloud White" is perceived by some customers as closer to cream or light khaki, not pure white.


### 5. Sizing confusion
The unisex chart generates a high volume of pre-purchase queries, especially at the Medium/Large boundary. Customers don't know if sizes listed are men's or women's.


### 6. Delivery speed
Australia Post delays frustrate customers. Comparison to Amazon's delivery speed is explicitly mentioned.

## 17. Frequently Asked Questions


### Who are Summit Alpaca Socks for?
Hikers, trade workers, outdoor adventurers, and anyone who values comfort, durability, and all-day freshness. Also suitable for people with sensitive skin (no lanolin).


### Why are alpaca socks better than wool or cotton?
Warmer than wool, 3× more breathable, naturally odour-resistant and antibacterial, moisture-wicking, hypoallergenic (no lanolin), and more durable.


### Are Summit Socks diabetic-friendly?
The socks are not specifically marketed as diabetic socks, but many customers with diabetes or circulation issues find them comfortable and suitable. The alpaca fibre is soft, non-irritating, and hypoallergenic. The design avoids harsh elastic bands. However, they do have a ribbed cuff that holds them in place. Customers with specific medical needs should consult their doctor.


### Can I mix and match styles/colours/sizes for the bundle discount?
Yes. Different sizes, colours, and styles can be combined to unlock bundle pricing.


### What size should I get if I'm between sizes?
Go with the larger size. Alpaca fibre has natural stretch for a comfortable fit.


### Do the socks shed or pill?
Some loose fibre shedding is normal in the first few wears. This settles after a couple of washes and does not affect the sock's performance or durability.


### How do I wash my Summit Socks?
Machine wash cold, gentle cycle, mild detergent. No fabric softener or bleach. Lay flat to dry. They don't need washing after every wear thanks to natural odour resistance.


### Where are Summit Socks made?
The socks are manufactured from premium Peruvian baby alpaca yarn.


### Do you ship internationally?
Australia is the primary market with free shipping over $89. New Zealand is also supported. Other international enquiries are handled case-by-case via hello@summitsocks.com.au.
